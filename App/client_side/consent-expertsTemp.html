CONSENT TO PARTICIPATE IN THE RESEARCH STUDY:

RANKING CODE SNIPPETS BY COMPREHENSION DIFFICULTY

I, , have been asked to participate in a research study under the direction of Dr. Martin Kellogg from the New Jersey Institute OF Technology (NJIT) and Dr. Oscar Chaparro from the College of William & Mary (W&M).

PURPOSE

The overarching goal of our research is to investigate the effectiveness of existing code comprehension measures in assessing the difficulty in understanding Java code snippets (i.e., code comprehensibility). Specifically, this research aims to determine whether these measures can reliably predict expert-determined rankings of code comprehensibility, contributing to improved methods for studying code comprehension.

The specific goal of this study is to establish an expert-determined ranking of 8 Java code snippets according to how difficulty they are for experts to understand.

DURATION

My participation in this study will consist of three rounds. Round 1 is expected to take about 75 minutes, Round 2 about 45 minutes, and Round 3 (a chat discussion phase) about 40 minutes. I will have up to one week to complete each round on my own schedule before the deadline.

BENEFITS FOR SOCIETY AND THE SUBJECT

I have been told that the benefits of this research include advancing knowledge on how code is understood by developers, which can advance teaching and research in software engineering. As a participant, I will have the opportunity to contribute my experience and knowledge to code comprehension research and receive a $50 Amazon gift card as compensation for participating in the study. In the event of early withdrawal, I will receive partial compensation proportional to the extent of my participation.

PROCEDURES

I have been told that, during this study, the following will occur:

  • I will participate as one of five expert Java developers in the study. My participation will take place through a secure web-based application (https://codecomprehensibility.site).
  • I will take part in three rounds, following a Delphi-style method designed to collect and compare expert judgments about the comprehensibility of Java code snippets.
  • In Round 1, I will log in using a dedicated username and password. For each of eight Java code snippets, I will (1) provide a short description of its functionality, (2) assess how understandable I find it, and (3) rank all eight snippets from easiest to hardest to understand using a drag-and-drop interface. I will also explain my reasoning.
  • I may revise my responses until I submit them, and I will have up to one week to complete each round before the deadline.
  • After each round, the research team will share de-identified feedback and reasoning from other experts. In Round 2, I will review this feedback, reflect on points of disagreement, and, if I wish, update my rankings and explanations.
  • Round 3 will be a chat-based discussion phase, where I can review prior feedback, participate in focused discussions with other experts, and, if I wish, update my ranking.
  • If no agreement is reached by the end of Round 3, a statistical aggregation method will be applied to produce the final expert-informed ranking.

PARTICIPANTS

I will be one of about five participants in this study.

EXCLUSIONS

I will inform the researcher if any of the following apply:

  • I am under 18 years of age.
  • I have been diagnosed with a reading or learning disability, or another condition that affects my ability to read or understand code or instructions.
  • I am not comfortable reading English text.
  • I have not received a significant promotion in a software engineering role (e.g., from entry level to a more independent role such as Apple ICT3, Facebook E4, Uber L4, Microsoft 61, Amazon L5, or Walmart L3).
  • I have no professional experience with Java.
  • I have not mentored at least one junior software engineer in a professional setting.

RISKS/DISCOMFORTS

This study may involve mild discomfort or fatigue from focusing for extended periods. To mitigate this, breaks will be instructed during the study. There may be unknown risks. I understand that I am not covered by our institutions' insurance policy for any injury or loss during participation.

CONFIDENTIALITY

I understand that my participation is confidential but not anonymous. My responses will be linked to my name and email so the research team can contact me. Responses may be shared (after de-identification) with other experts but not linked to my identity. All data will be stored securely on AWS during the study and then transferred to our local servers and deleted from AWS. Only the research team will access identifying information.

I will receive a $50 Amazon gift card and a certificate of participation from NJIT and William & Mary after completing the study.

RIGHT TO REFUSE OR WITHDRAW
